Do you love the internet?

We do. Which is why we spend a lot of time working on something we here at Mozilla like to call Internet Health—making the internet safe, secure, fun, and weird for people all around the world.

Every year in April we put out a report to the world—the Internet Health Report—detailing what's going well on the internet, and what needs work to make the internet better. Since the internet impacts all our lives, we'd love to hear ideas from you. What sorts of stories or articles or research have you seen lately that inspires you or scares you about the internet these days?

Submit your ideas before September 14th.

Take a look at this year's Internet Health Report. If you see anything missing or anything that needs more attention, let us know and we'll work to include it in the 2019 Internet Health Report. That report will be used by experts, researchers, activists, and journalists to help shape the conversation about the internet as we move forward.

So get your ideas over to us before September 14th. Working for a healthier internet is a team effort. Thanks for being part of the team.

Best,

Solana Larsen
Editor, Internet Health Report

PS. You can also submit ideas to us on Twitter. Just tweet them out and use the hashtag #internethealth.
